Reserve a Day Use Pass

The BC Parks Day Use Pilot Project is continuing for the 2021 winter season to help manage visitation levels at Mount Seymour Provincial Park, one of British Columbia's most popular winter day use parks.

Visitors are now required to reserve an a.m. or p.m. day use parking pass for their visit to Mount Seymour, as this park will have no available first come, first serve parking during the day (7:00am–4:00pm) from December 1, 2021 to March 31, 2022.

Important Booking Information:
  • Bookings can be made starting at 7:00am, two days in advance of your planned visit.
  • Passes will remain available until capacity is met — additional passes may be released each day at 7:00am, dependent on weather conditions.
  • Passes cannot be transferred to another date, time, or location and your pass cannot be shared beyond the group reserved.
  • The A.M. pass permits parking from 7:00am to noon. The P.M. pass starts at noon and reserves a spot until 4:00pm. After 4:00pm, day-use parking spots will be claimed on a first come, first serve basis.
Day Use Vehicle Pass:
  • 1 pass required per vehicle. Maximum of 8 persons (adult or child) per pass per day.
  • Maximum of one A.M. and one P.M. vehicle pass can be booked per account, per day.
Keys to Visiting the Great Outdoors:
  • Plan ahead. Download and use the AdventureSmart trip planning app.
  • Nature is unpredictable; do your own research and pack the essentials - be prepared for anything.
  • Share your plan with a friend. Let them know where you are going and when you expect to be back.
  • Pack out all garbage, recyclables, and organics. Organic matter can attract wildlife.
  • Always travel within your abilities and prepare for your trip. Follow in-park signage and have your pass accessible while in the park and on the trail.

For more information on the day pass pilot program, including what to expect in park, please visit us at https://bcparks.ca/reserve/day-use/.
