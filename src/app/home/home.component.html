Reserve a Day Use Pass

The BC Parks Day Use Pilot Project is continuing for the 2021 winter season to help manage visitation levels at Mount Seymour Provincial Park, one of British Columbia's most popular winter day use parks.

Visitors who intend on using the backcountry trail network at Mount Seymour Park are now required to reserve an a.m. or p.m. day use parking pass for their visit, as there will be no available first come, first serve parking during the day (7:00am–4:00pm) from December 7, 2021 to March 31, 2022.

Vehicle Pass
  • 1 pass required per vehicle. Maximum of 8 persons (adult or child) per pass per day.
  • Maximum of one A.M. and one P.M. vehicle pass can be booked per account, per day.

  • Bookings can be made starting at 7:00am, two days in advance of your planned visit.
  • Passes will remain available until capacity is met — additional passes may be released each day at 7:00am, dependent on weather conditions.
  • Passes cannot be transferred to another date, time, or location and your pass cannot be shared beyond the group reserved.
  • The A.M. pass permits parking from 7:00am to noon. The P.M. pass starts at noon and reserves a spot until 4:00pm. After 4:00pm, day-use parking spots will be claimed on a first come, first serve basis.

Keys to Visiting the Great Outdoors

1

Trip plan

Check bcparks.ca for updates. Know what the weather and conditions will be. Download and use AdventureSmart's Trip Plan app to share your plan with a trusted contact.

2

Train

Obtain knowledge and skills you need before heading out. Know and stay within your limits.

3

Take the essentials

Pack the basic survival items you should have in any outdoor situation

4

Responsible Recreation

Parks aren't self cleaning. Leave natural and cultural objects undisturbed. Keep pets on leash. Nice people make nature nicer. Learn more bcparks.ca/beresponsible.

5

Keep wildlife wild

Wild animals are supposed to eat wild things. Please give wildlife space and store your food properly.

6

Enjoy your visit

Enjoy your visit For more information on the day pass pilot program, including what to expect in park, please visit us at bcparks.ca/reserve/day-use/ .
